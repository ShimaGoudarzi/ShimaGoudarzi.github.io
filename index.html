About

Shima Goudarzi is a senior researcher and lecturer at the institut of Renewable Energy Systems at the University Of Applied Scieneces Vienna (Technikum Wien). She studied mechanical engineering and received her PhD from the Vienna University of Technology (TU Wien) in 2014.

She also has six years of experience as a technical manager at a building energy planning company in Austria, focusing on thermally activated buildings (Baueilaktivierung). She and her group were awarded the 2019 State Prize for Sustainability (Staatspreis Architektur und Nachhaltigkeit) as one of the most prestigious prizes in Austria.

She therefore is familiar with the most bleeding edge developments in the sustainable building industry.
Furthermore she has research experience in modeling energy systems and analyzing energy in buildings, as well as hands-on experience in designing and simulating them using various tools.

Her current research interests are: Energy efficiency in buildings, plus-energy buildings and neighborhoods, buildings energy planning, thermaly activated buildings.

Other research areas include:

Energy, energy poverty, sustainable building, urban development, digital technologies in cities & buildings, urban designing, urban planning, smart cities and plus energy districts.
