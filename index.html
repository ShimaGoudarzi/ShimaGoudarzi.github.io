About

Shima Goudarzi is a senior researcher and lecturer at the Institute of Renewable Energy Systems at the University of Applied Sciences Vienna (Technikum Wien).

She received her PhD in mechanical engineering from the Vienna University of Technology (TU Wien) in 2014. In addition, Dr. Goudarzi has six years of experience as a technical manager at a building energy planning company in Austria, specializing in thermally activated buildings (Baueilaktivierung).
Her group was awarded the prestigious 2019 State Prize for Sustainability (Staatspreis Architektur und Nachhaltigkeit) in Austria.

Dr. Goudarzi has expertise in the latest developments in the sustainable building industry, as well as research experience in modeling and analyzing energy systems in buildings and hands-on experience in designing and simulating them using various tools.

Research Interests

Dr. Goudarzi's research interests span a wide range of topics related to renewable energy and sustainable development, including:

Energy efficiency in buildings, energy planning and the construction of thermally activated buildings, sustainable urban planning and design, plus-energy districts that produce excess energy, and the use of digital technologies in cities and buildings to monitor and optimize energy usage.

With a focus on innovative and practical solutions, her research is of great importance in addressing the current global energy crisis and finding sustainable solutions for the future.
